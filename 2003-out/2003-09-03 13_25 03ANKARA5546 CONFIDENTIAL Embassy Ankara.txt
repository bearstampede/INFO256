Begin Text: 
 
 
OAGY-II/273 dated September 2, 2003. 
 
 
The Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the Republic of Turkey 
presents its compliments to the Embassy of the United States 
of America and with reference to Ministry's Note No. 
OAGY-II/400, dated 20 August 2003 has the honor to inform the 
esteemed Embassy that our delegation in Iraq will return to 
Turkey on the 4th of September upon the completion of its 
meetings with various Iraqi tribal groups and the CPA 
officials. 
 
 
The delegation will be transported back from Baghdad by a 
Casa type aircraft of the Turkish Air Force.  The information 
pertaining to the flight as well as the list of weapons and 
ammunition that will be in the possession of the delegation 
are attached herewith. 
 
 
The Ministry would like to inform the Embassy that in 
addition to the delegation, the spouse of our Ambassador in 
Baghdad and three Embassy staff will also be on board.  The 
TAF aircraft will also be carrying the daughter of Sheikh 
Zavi Al-Gireyri, the leader of the Al-Janabe tribe who will 
be taken to Gulhane Military Hospital (GATA) for medical 
treatment. 
 
 
From Ankara to Baghdad, the aircraft will be transporting 
four administrative attaches who are posted as security staff 
at the Turkish Embassy, Baghdad.  The Ministry has the honor 
to present herewith the list of the names of these personnel, 
as well as their weapons. 
 
 
The Ministry requests the esteemed Embassy to kindly liaise 
with the US forces in Baghdad and CENTCOM with a view to 
obtaining landing and take-off clearances for TAF aircraft to 
Baghdad and ensuring secure transfer of the delegation to the 
Baghdad International Airport from the Turkish Embassy. 
 
 
The Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the Republic of Turkey 
avails itself of this opportunity to renew the Embassy of the 
United States of America the assurance of its highest 
consideration. 
 
 
Ankara, 2 September, 2003. 
 
 
end text of diplomatic note.

¶4. (C) Embassy requests the following assistance: 
 
 
-- confirmation that the Turkish Aircraft is cleared to land 
at BIAP, 
-- confirmation that Turkish Embassy vehicles will be allowed 
to enter BIAP to deliver the delegation to the aircraft, OR 
that transportation to the airport from the Turkish Embassy 
will be provided, 
-- confirmation that the delegation will have safe and 
unhindered travel out of Baghdad, and 
-- information on a US emergency point of contact in Baghdad.
