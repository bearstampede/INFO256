SUBJECT: CHR: ITALIANS DON'T FAVOR UZBEK RESOLUTION 
 
REF: STATE 50850 
 
                       UNCLASSIFIED 
 
PAGE 02        ROME  00830  271509Z 
¶1. (U) MFA HUMAN RIGHTS DIRECTOR CALVETTA TOLD US FEB 27 THAT 
ITALY AGREED A RESOLUTION ON UZBEKISTAN WAS NOT TIMELY AT 
THIS YEAR'S HUMAN RIGHTS COMMISSION.  CALVETTA AGREED THAT 
UZBEKISTAN'S IMPLEMENTATION OF THE SPECIAL RAPPORTEUR'S 
RECOMMENDATIONS WAS THE APPROPRIATE TEST OF THE ITS PROFESSED 
COMMITMENT TO IMPROVED PERFORMANCE ON HUMAN RIGHTS ISSUES. 
IF TASHKENT FAILED THAT TEST, HOWEVER, WE WOULD NEED TO SEND 
A STRONG SIGNAL THROUGH A RESOLUTION AT NEXT YEAR'S CHR, 
CALVETTA ADDED. 
 
¶2. (SBU) CALVETTA CONCLUDED BY OBSERVING THAT MOST OTHER EU 
MEMBER-STATES SHARED ITALY'S ANALYSIS AND AGREED WITH THE 
U.S. SUGGESTION TO DEFER A RESOLUTION THIS YEAR.
