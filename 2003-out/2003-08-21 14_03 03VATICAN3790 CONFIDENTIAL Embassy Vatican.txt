¶1.  (C) DCM MET WITH HOLY SEE DEPUTY FOREIGN MINISTER PIETRO 
PAROLIN AUGUST 20 TO REVIEW U.S. THEMES FOR THE 58TH UNGA 
(REFTELS).  IN A BRIEF EXCHANGE ON THE SITUATION IN IRAQ, 
    PAROLIN EXPRESSED SADNESS AT THE RECENT ATTACK, AND WAS 
RECEPTIVE TO USG DETERMINATION TO STAY THE COURSE.  HE NOTED 
OUR POINT THAT IRAQ ISSUES SHOULD REMAIN IN THE SECURITY 
COUNCIL.  ON SUPPORT FOR MIDDLE EAST PEACE, PAROLIN INDICATED 
THAT THE HOLY SEE CONTINUES TO STRONGLY SUPPORT U.S. EFFORTS 
THROUGH THE ROADMAP.  REGARDING OUR CONCERNS REGARDING THE 
INSERTION OF REFERENCES TO THE INTERNATIONAL CRIMINAL COURT 
(ICC) IN UNRELATED UN RESOLUTIONS, AND OUR MESSAGE ON THE 
POTENTIAL FOR UNGA TO ENERGIZE THE INTERNATIONAL COMMUNITY ON 
COUNTERTERRORISM ISSUES, PAROLIN LISTENED ATTENTIVELY AND 
TOOK OUR POINTS ON BOARD.  HE WAS RECEPTIVE TO THE USG'S 
DESIRE FOR HUMAN RIGHTS RESOLUTIONS ON THE COUNTRIES 
MENTIONED IN REF B, AND MENTIONED THE VATICAN'S PARTICULAR 
CONCERN ABOUT THE HUMAN RIGHTS SITUATION IN ZIMBABWE. 
 
¶2.  (C) ON ALL ISSUES, PAROLIN NOTED THAT THE HOLY SEE'S 
PERMANENT OBSERVER MISSION IN NEW YORK WOULD PLAY A KEY ROLE 
IN GUIDING THE HOLY SEE'S APPROACH, AND AGREED TO PASS ALONG 
TO THE MISSION THE U.S. PRIORITIES.
