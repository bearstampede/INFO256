SUBJECT: ITALY CAN NOT ASSIST IN CLEARING AFGHANISTAN'S IFI

THE GOI OFFICIALLY INFORMED US TODAY THAT ITALY WILL NOT BE 
ABLE TO PROVIDE ADDITIONAL FUNDING TO HELP AFGHANISTAN CLEAR 
ARREARS. GIORGIO LECCESI, DIRECTOR, INTERNATIONAL RELATIONS 
OFFICE OF THE FINANCE MINISTRY, AND COUNSELOR ALESSANDRO 
GAUDIANO OF THE MINISTRY OF FOREIGN AFFAIRS TOLD A/ECOUN THAT 
A NEW LEGISLATIVE DECREE WOULD BE REQUIRED FOR AUTHORIZATION 
OF NEW ASSISTANCE FUNDING. APPROVAL OF SUCH A DECREE WOULD 
TAKE WEEKS, IF NOT MONTHS. IN DECEMBER 2002, THE GOI 
AUTHORIZED AND DISBURSED E3.2 MILLION TO THE WORLD BANK TO 
ASSIST AFGHANISTAN. ITALY'S FUNDING COMMITMENT WAS ANNOUNCED 
AT THE AFGHAN DONORS' CONFERENCE IN OSLO, DECEMBER 17-18.
